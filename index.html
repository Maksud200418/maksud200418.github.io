Gorilla
Scientific classification Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Mammalia
Order: Primates
Family: Hominidae
Genus: Gorilla

Introduction

Gorillas are the largest living primates and share around 98% of their DNA with humans. They live in the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa and are known for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and gentle nature.

Diet and Behavior

Gorillas primarily eat a vegetarian diet consisting of fruits, leaves, and stems, though some may consume small insects. They live in groups led by a dominant male called a silverback, who protects the group and makes key decisions.

Intelligence

These primates are highly intelligent and have been observed using tools in the wild. Some gorillas in captivity have even been taught sign language to communicate with humans. Their populations are threatened by habitat destruction, poaching, and disease, making conservation efforts crucial for their survival.

Social Structure

Gorillas display a wide range of emotions, such as joy, sadness, and even laughter. They are known to form strong bonds within their groups, and their social structures resemble those of humans in many ways.
